Follow Up....

Thi s is where all the “Problem Statements” are banked. Go in, read through them. Find one you think you will like to handle and the work on it.

1. Submission of Issues:- Teachers submit their manual processes or challenges to the Bank, known as “Problem Statements”. This can include any repetitive tasks, paperwork, or administrative duties (that is experienced in the Industry) that they believe could be streamlined through technology. 

2. Review and Selection:- The submitted issues are reviewed by the TechSavvy Society team to assess their feasibility and potential for digitization or automation. Priority may be given to issues with a high impact on efficiency or effectiveness. 

3. Brainstorming and Ideation:- Once published in the Members' Forum, Students can select which Problem Statement they would like to take on and then begin with brainstorming innovative solutions to the identified challenges. They explore various digital tools, technologies, and approaches to propose effective and efficient solutions.  

4. Documentation and Presentation:- Selected ideas are further developed into detailed documentation and presentations. Students outline the proposed digital solutions, including workflows, functionalities, and potential benefits. Tools can be anything online - free or paid (The Club will bare no expenses) and the creation of a prototype model presented would make it really stand out. 
The finalized solutions are converted into actionable tasks along with any links, documented and sent from your @mycahm.onmicrosoft.com email account and then emailing them to club.techsavvy@cahm.lk 
The Club will not own any of the projects and solutions but will have rights to freely use them in any form or manner and cannot be held accountable for using intellectual property of the Student even after the student author graduates and works in industry.
